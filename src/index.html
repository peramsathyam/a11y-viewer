Vision loss

Worldwide 285 million people are estimated to be visually impaired

World Health Organization

Visual impairment, or simply vision loss, is a decreased ability to see. Common types of vision loss are near-sightedness orfar-sightedness. The most severe form is complete vision loss, or simply blindness.

Type of vision loss Number of people Percentage
Low vision 246 million 86%
Blindness 39 million 14%

Many types of vision loss can be prevented or cured with treatment. Vision loss cannot be fixed with glasses or contact lenses.

Pointers and touch

People with severe or complete vision loss navigate web pages using a keyboard or braille keyboard. They may not be able to use a pointer device (e.g. mouse) or interact with mobile devices using touch gestures. This means they interact with a web page differently than most people with normal vision.

Color blindness

Roughly 8% men and 0.5% women with Northern European ancestry are color blind

color-blindness.com

Color blindness ranges from almost normal color vision to not being able to distinguish colors at all. The term color blindness is misleading: practically all color blind people are not completely color blind, but are color deficient. Color deficient people are unable to distinguish certain colors.

The most common form of color deficiency is red-green blindness. Red-green blindness is a combination of red blindness (protan effects) and green blindness (deutan effects).

Red-green blindness is encoded on the X sex chromosome. This means color blindness is prevalent among males, and not females.
